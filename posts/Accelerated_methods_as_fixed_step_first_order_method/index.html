Accelerated first-order methods as fixed-step first-order methods: conversion between momentum form and standard form

Shuvomoy Das Gupta

August 06, 2021

In this blog, we discuss how to write accelerated first-order methods as fixed-step first-order methods.

Converting between momentum-form and and standard form of fixed-step first-order method

Consider a LLL-smooth function. ​Fixed step first order methods for this function fff are algorithms of the form (called the standard form)

xk=x0−∑j=0k−1hk,jL∇f(xj),(FSFOM)x_{k}=x_{0}-\sum_{j=0}^{k-1}\frac{h_{k,j}}{L}\nabla f(x_{j}),\quad\textrm{(FSFOM)}xk​=x0​−j=0∑k−1​Lhk,j​​∇f(xj​),(FSFOM)

​​​ where k∈{0,1,…,N}k\in\{0,1,\ldots,N\}k∈{0,1,…,N}​​​.

Also, we have the following "momentum form" of the fixed-step first-order method:

yi+1=xi−1L∇f(xi)xi+1=yi+1+ζi+1(yi+1−yi)+ηi+1(yi+1−xi),(MomentumOGM) \begin{array}{ll} y_{i+1} & =x_{i}-\frac{1}{L}\nabla f(x_{i})\\ x_{i+1} & =y_{i+1}+\zeta_{i+1}(y_{i+1}-y_{i})+\eta_{i+1}(y_{i+1}-x_{i}), \end{array}\quad(\textrm{MomentumOGM)} yi+1​xi+1​​=xi​−L1​∇f(xi​)=yi+1​+ζi+1​(yi+1​−yi​)+ηi+1​(yi+1​−xi​),​(MomentumOGM)

which we show to be equivalent to (FSFOM). To show that (MomentumOGM) is in the form (FSFOM), we put, the iterative form y+1y_{+1}y+1​​​ and yiy_{i}yi​​​ in terms of the xxx​​ iterates in the second iterate. For simplification purpose, denote gi=∇f(xi)g_{i}=\nabla f(x_{i})gi​=∇f(xi​)​​​. We get:

xi+1=xi+ζi+1(xi−xi−1)−(ζi+1+ηi+1+1)Lgi+ζi+1Lgi−1,(MOM-SIMP) x_{i+1}=x_{i}+\zeta_{i+1}\left(x_{i}-x_{i-1}\right)-\frac{\left(\zeta_{i+1}+\eta_{i+1}+1\right)}{L}g_{i}+\frac{\zeta_{i+1}}{L}g_{i-1},\quad \textrm{(MOM-SIMP)} xi+1​=xi​+ζi+1​(xi​−xi−1​)−L(ζi+1​+ηi+1​+1)​gi​+Lζi+1​​gi−1​,(MOM-SIMP)

where the Mathematica code for this is shown below.

Now from (FSFOM):

xi=x0−∑j=0i−1hi,jLgj,xi−1=x0−∑j=0i−2hi−1,jLgj,\begin{aligned} x_{i} & =x_{0}-\sum_{j=0}^{i-1}\frac{h_{i,j}}{L}g_{j},\\ x_{i-1} & =x_{0}-\sum_{j=0}^{i-2}\frac{h_{i-1,j}}{L}g_{j},\end{aligned}xi​xi−1​​=x0​−j=0∑i−1​Lhi,j​​gj​,=x0​−j=0∑i−2​Lhi−1,j​​gj​,​

​​ which gives

xi−xi−1=−∑j=0i−2(−hi−1,j+hi,j)Lgj−hi,i−1Lgi−1(Diff-x) \begin{aligned} x_{i}-x_{i-1} & =-\sum_{j=0}^{i-2}\frac{(-h_{i-1,j}+h_{i,j})}{L}g_{j}-\frac{h_{i,i-1}}{L}g_{i-1}\quad \textrm{(Diff-x)} \end{aligned} xi​−xi−1​​=−j=0∑i−2​L(−hi−1,j​+hi,j​)​gj​−Lhi,i−1​​gi−1​(Diff-x)​

and putting this in (MOM-SIMP) and then simplifying we get:

xi+1=xi−ζi+1∑j=0i−2(hi,j−hi−1,j)Lgj−ζi+1(hi,i−1−1)Lgi−1−(ζi+1+ηi+1+1)Lgi,(FSFOM-A) \begin{align*} & x_{i+1}\\ = & x_{i}-\zeta_{i+1}\sum_{j=0}^{i-2}\frac{\left(h_{i,j}-h_{i-1,j}\right)}{L}g_{j}-\frac{\zeta_{i+1}\left(h_{i,i-1}-1\right)}{L}g_{i-1}-\frac{\left(\zeta_{i+1}+\eta_{i+1}+1\right)}{L}g_{i}, \end{align*} \quad \textrm{(FSFOM-A)} =​xi+1​xi​−ζi+1​j=0∑i−2​L(hi,j​−hi−1,j​)​gj​−Lζi+1​(hi,i−1​−1)​gi−1​−L(ζi+1​+ηi+1​+1)​gi​,​(FSFOM-A)

xi+1=xi−∑j=0i−1(hi+1,j−hi,j)Lgj−hi+1,iLgi(Diff-x-2)x_{i+1}=x_{i}-\sum_{j=0}^{i-1}\frac{(h_{i+1,j}-h_{i,j})}{L}g_{j}-\frac{h_{i+1,i}}{L}g_{i}\quad \textrm{(Diff-x-2)}xi+1​=xi​−j=0∑i−1​L(hi+1,j​−hi,j​)​gj​−Lhi+1,i​​gi​(Diff-x-2)

Note that (Diff-x-2) and (FSFOM-A) are in the same format now for a pattern matching. Comparing the terms part by part, we get the following recursive system: ​

with initial condition h1,j=0h_{1,j}=0h1,j​=0​​​ for j<0j<0j<0​​​ and h0,j=0h_{0,j}=0h0,j​=0​​​ for all jjj​​​. This system of equation gives us a way to compute ζ,η\zeta,\etaζ,η​ from hhh​.

Specific example: OGM

As our example, we consider the Optimized Gradient Method (OGM) due to Kim and Fessler. For a LLL-smooth convex function fff, x0∈Rd,θ0=1,x_{0}\in\mathbf{R}^{d},\theta_{0}=1,x0​∈Rd,θ0​=1, the algorithm is defined in its auxiliary form as

yi+1=xi−1L∇f(xi)zi+1=zi−2θiL∇f(xi)xi+1=(1−1θi+1)yi+1+1θi+1zi+1,(OGM)\begin{array}{ll} y_{i+1} & =x_{i}-\frac{1}{L}\nabla f(x_{i})\\ z_{i+1} & =z_{i}-\frac{2\theta_{i}}{L}\nabla f(x_{i})\\ x_{i+1} & =\left(1-\frac{1}{\theta_{i+1}}\right)y_{i+1}+\frac{1}{\theta_{i+1}}z_{i+1}, \end{array}\quad(\textrm{OGM)}yi+1​zi+1​xi+1​​=xi​−L1​∇f(xi​)=zi​−L2θi​​∇f(xi​)=(1−θi+1​1​)yi+1​+θi+1​1​zi+1​,​(OGM)

​ where i∈{0,1,…,N−1}i\in\{0,1,\ldots,N-1\}i∈{0,1,…,N−1}​.

First, note that in (OGM), gradient is evaluated at xix_{i}xi​​ iterates, so we will try to remove ziz_{i}zi​​ iterates from (OGM), and write the last iterate as terms involving yi+1,yi,y_{i+1},y_{i},yi+1​,yi​,​ and xix_{i}xi​​. To that goal, we will write, zi+1z_{i+1}zi+1​​ completely using yi,xi,y_{i},x_{i},yi​,xi​,​ and yi+1y_{i+1}yi+1​​. From, the first iteration of (OGM),

1L∇f(xi)=xi−yi+1,(1)\frac{1}{L}\nabla f(x_{i})=x_{i}-y_{i+1},\quad(1)L1​∇f(xi​)=xi​−yi+1​,(1)

zi+1=zi−2θiL∇f(xi)=zi−(2θi)(xi−yi+1)=zi−2θixi+2θiyi+1(2).\begin{aligned} z_{i+1} & =z_{i}-\frac{2\theta_{i}}{L}\nabla f(x_{i})\\ & =z_{i}-(2\theta_{i})(x_{i}-y_{i+1})\\ & =z_{i}-2\theta_{i}x_{i}+2\theta_{i}y_{i+1}\quad(2).\end{aligned}zi+1​​=zi​−L2θi​​∇f(xi​)=zi​−(2θi​)(xi​−yi+1​)=zi​−2θi​xi​+2θi​yi+1​(2).​
